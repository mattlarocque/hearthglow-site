Privacy Notice

Effective date: [CONFIRM — e.g. October 1, 2026] · Last updated: [CONFIRM]

Hearthglow ("we," "us," "our") provides seasonal display installation and rental services in Ontario. We respect your privacy and handle your personal information in accordance with Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL). This notice explains what we collect, why, how we store and protect it, who we share it with, and the rights you have over it.

1. Who is responsible for your information

Hearthglow is the organization accountable for the personal information in its custody and control. Questions, requests, or complaints can be directed to our Privacy Officer (see Contact us below).

2. What we collect

  • Contact and quote details — your name, email address, phone number, and the property address you ask us to service.
  • Service preferences — the service tier you're interested in, preferred install window, and any notes you provide.
  • Photos you upload — images of your property used to measure and prepare your quote.
  • Quote, agreement, and payment records — your quote, the agreement you accept, invoice and deposit amounts, and payment status. We do not store full payment-card numbers; card payments are handled by our payment processor.
  • Consent records — a record of the consents you give (for example, to receive quote updates or marketing email), as required by CASL.
  • Limited technical information — such as the IP address and timestamp recorded when you submit a request or accept an agreement, used for security and to evidence consent.

3. Why we collect it and our legal basis

We use your personal information to:

  • respond to your request, prepare and deliver your quote, and confirm your service area;
  • perform the agreement — schedule installation, collect deposits, issue invoices, and arrange teardown;
  • operate your customer portal account (a passwordless login link sent to your email);
  • send you transactional messages about your quote, agreement, payments, and bookings;
  • send you marketing email only if you have consented, and meet our legal, tax, and accounting obligations.

We collect, use, and disclose your information with your consent. Transactional messages (about a quote or service you requested) are sent under CASL's implied/transactional provisions. Marketing messages are sent only with your express consent, which you can withdraw at any time using the unsubscribe link in any marketing email.

4. Where your information is stored (data residency)

We store customer information on servers located in Canada (the AWS Canada (Central) region, ca-central-1). Our policy is to use Canadian-incorporated service providers and Canadian data centres wherever possible. Certain supporting infrastructure (currently Amazon Web Services for email delivery and photo storage, in the Canadian region) is used under our data-sovereignty policy and is being transitioned to Canadian-sovereign alternatives as we grow. Where any provider may be subject to foreign law, we limit what is shared and protect it contractually.

5. Who we share it with

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only as needed to provide the service:

  • Service providers (processors) — for email delivery, secure photo storage, and payment processing, under agreements that limit them to processing data on our instructions.
  • Subcontractors — installation crews receive the limited details needed to perform your job (such as your service address and scope).
  • Legal and regulatory — where required by law, or to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

6. How long we keep it

We keep your personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes above and to meet legal, tax, and accounting requirements (for example, invoices and contract records are retained for the period required by law — typically [CONFIRM — e.g. 6–7 years]). When it is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize it.

7. Your rights

Subject to legal limits, you may:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you and ask how it has been used or disclosed;
  • Correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete;
  • Withdraw consent at any time (note this may mean we can no longer provide the service);
  • Unsubscribe from marketing email at any time;
  • Request erasure of your personal information where we are not required to retain it.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Privacy Officer below. We will respond within the timeframe required by PIPEDA (generally 30 days).

8. How we protect your information

We use technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information, including encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS), access controls, passwordless login links that expire, and storage limited to authorized systems. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your information and to address any incident promptly.

9. Children

Our services are directed to property owners and are not intended for children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Changes to this notice

We may update this notice from time to time. The "Last updated" date above reflects the most recent version. Material changes will be communicated where appropriate.
